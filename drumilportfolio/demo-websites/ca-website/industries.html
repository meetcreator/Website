Client Sectors

Industries Served

Different sectors carry different compliance obligations. Our practice spans a broad range of industries — each with its own regulatory considerations under direct tax, GST, and corporate law frameworks.

Sectors We Serve

Our Client Base

Manufacturing & MSMEs

GST on goods, excise carry-overs, input credit reconciliation, factory audit, MSME registration, and export incentive claims are common requirements for this sector.

Trading & Retail

Stock audit, TCS on sales, GST rate classification, and reconciliation of purchases with GSTR-2B are routine compliance areas for trading entities.

Real Estate & Construction

GST on under-construction properties, TDS on immovable property purchases, capital gains on property transactions, and JDA tax treatment require careful attention.

Healthcare & Pharmaceuticals

Healthcare entities navigate GST exemptions, transfer pricing on pharmaceutical imports, and specific professional tax considerations for doctors in practice.

Information Technology & Software

Export of services, STPI / SEZ compliance, equity compensation (ESOP) taxation, and software subscription GST classification are key areas for IT firms.

Startups & Emerging Businesses

DPIIT registration, Section 80-IAC income tax exemption, angel tax provisions, ESOP valuation, and early-stage structuring are typical requirements.

Textile & Garments

The textile sector in Gujarat has specific GST rate complexities, job-work provisions under Section 143, and seasonal compliance cycles.

Education & Non-Profit

Section 12A / 80G registration, FCRA compliance for foreign donations, and income tax exemption maintenance for charitable institutions.

Professionals (Doctors, Lawyers, Architects)

Presumptive income under Section 44ADA, advance tax planning, professional tax, and home office deduction advisory for independent practitioners.

NRI & Overseas Clients

Indian-source income tax compliance, repatriation of assets, FEMA reporting, and DTAA benefit claims for non-resident Indians and foreign nationals.

A Note on Scope

Sector knowledge matters in compliance.

Regulations often interact in ways specific to a sector — a rate exemption under GST may affect ITC eligibility; an export benefit may trigger FEMA reporting. We apply cross-functional awareness to each client engagement.

If your sector is not listed here, we welcome an initial conversation. Our practice has handled varied and uncommon compliance scenarios across our years in operation.
